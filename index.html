About Me

Building AI that actually ships

Jacob is a software engineer at Microsoft building AI infrastructure on Azure — systems trusted by OpenAI, BlackRock, and Krafton to keep critical workloads running at scale. His work spans predictive failure modeling for 14,000+ containers per day, a host health observability platform built in direct response to customer escalations, and a 6-team networking pipeline project that drove incident root cause accuracy from 5% to 99% at global scale. Before Microsoft, he built NLP and intelligent search systems at Boeing and Capital One — cutting document retrieval time by 90% as part of Boeing's $10B efficiency initiative.

Outside his day job, Jacob independently builds and ships AI-powered products: a desktop automation tool that compresses 1–2 hours of tax document processing into under 5 minutes using AWS Bedrock, an autonomous overnight AI coding system that manages 6 projects in parallel, a live quantitative trading platform with real-time signal detection, and a full-stack social application built on Next.js and Supabase. He works across the full stack — cloud infrastructure on Azure and AWS, LLM integration, Python and TypeScript backends, and data pipelines.

Through Bedrock Technical Solutions, Jacob works with small businesses and teams to find where AI can replace hours of manual work — and then builds it. He brings the engineering depth to ship real solutions and the communication skills to make AI accessible to non-technical founders and operators. If you're wondering what AI could do for your business, start with a conversation.
